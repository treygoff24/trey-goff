# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Readers arriving from Trey's writing, policy work, and network: think-tank and governance people checking who he is, engineers evaluating his taste, editors and collaborators, and the occasionally curious. They come to read, browse the library, and form an impression. Mostly desktop-at-night and phone-in-transit contexts.

## Product Purpose

Trey Goff's personal site — writing, projects, library, and self — and explicitly an artistic collaboration between Trey and the Claude models he works with (Fable among them). Success is a visitor thinking "how was this made?" and leaving with a precise sense of the person: governance reformer, reader, builder. The Library is the signature surface; the rest of the site is a quiet, confident frame around the writing.

## Brand Personality

Literary, nocturnal, alive. A near-black emerald study lit by one aurora — a private observatory, not a portfolio. Serious about ideas, playful in the margins. Human–AI co-authorship is part of the identity, not a disclaimer.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev-portfolio grammar: card grids, hero metrics, "I build things for the web" heroes, skill badges.
- SaaS landing-page scaffolding: tracked-caps eyebrows on every section, numbered section markers, gradient text.
- The stock editorial-magazine template (italic display serif + mono labels + rules, no imagery). The site borrows editorial bones but the living aurora field and the Library are what keep it from being that template — never ship a surface that would survive with the aurora removed.
- Anything that reads "which AI made this?" instead of "how was this made?"

## Design Principles

1. **One living field.** A single global aurora layer carries the atmosphere; every other surface stays quiet so it can.
2. **Ruled, not carded.** Editorial rows and rules over card grids, except where a drawer/modal is the true affordance.
3. **One green, everywhere.** A single accent (#6FD69A → #97E8BB hover) carries every interaction; no second accent, ever.
4. **Two signature surfaces.** The Library (four lenses over everything read) holds the largest single interaction budget; the Workshop (four lenses over everything built) is its deliberate sibling. Every other page spends its budget on typography and pacing.
5. **Co-authored, and says so.** The human–AI collaboration is legible in the craft and, where natural, in the copy (colophon, about) — voice, not gimmick.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Dark-only theme, so contrast discipline matters: body text ≥4.5:1 against #04130C/#061A10 (secondary text via opacity must stay above the floor). `prefers-reduced-motion` fully honored — no continuous GPU/CPU loops for reduced-motion users; aurora falls back to the static asset. Full keyboard access to Library lenses and drawer.
